VR Spray Studio

A creative VR art space to practice spray‑painting directly on 3D objects—from simple shapes to complex sculptures—so artists can develop real techniques in an immersive, low‑stakes environment.

Project Snapshot

  • Platform: Unity 6
  • Interaction: Controller‑based spray can with pressure/flow; optional hand poses for grip
  • Focus: Technique drills on 3D targets (cubes, spheres, mannequins, car door, etc.)

What you'll do

Pick a 3D object, choose a nozzle, and paint. Simple as that.

About the Project

VR Spray Studio is a focused, technique‑first VR art experience. Instead of painting on flat canvases, you paint directly on three‑dimensional forms—like spheres, mannequins, and vehicle panels—so your strokes must follow real curves and edges. The goal is to help artists build muscle memory for line control, distance, angle, and motion.

Physically‑inspired spray model

Variable cone, falloff, and jitter mapped to trigger pressure; decals/projected paint that conforms to mesh normals.

Layers, stencils

Simple layer stack with blend modes; place stencils on surfaces

Milestones

  1. Spray shader + controller input (pressure/flow)
  2. Surface projection & UV‑correct decals
  3. Layers, stencils, and basic gallery
  4. Polish, UX pass, and performance (60 FPS)
